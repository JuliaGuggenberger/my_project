Welcome to the Experiment

In this experiment, you will make daily travel decisions for each day of a week. These weeks are based on the information you provided in your travel diary, reflecting your real commute.

To get started, you'll first complete two test weeks to become familiar with the process. After that, you'll move on to six main weeks, where your travel decisions will determine your payoff. The calculation of your payoff will be explained later.

Later in the experiment, you will also play simultaneously with other participants. In those rounds, you may need to wait until everyone has made their choices.

Tradable Credit Scheme Experiment

In this experiment, you will help us to test a scheme designed to reduce carbon dioxide (CO₂) emissions caused by using cars to travel to work. As part of this scheme, everyone will be given a certain number of Token for free. To use the car to travel to work and back home (commuting), you will need to spend a certain number of Token. The exact number of Token charged will depend on how much CO₂ you emit when using the car. Each Token represents 1 kg of CO₂ emissions. For example, if you travel during the peak period when there is more traffic congestion, you cause more emissions and hence, you will be charged more Token.
If you do not have sufficient Token with you but would still like to use the car, you can buy extra Token in a market at a certain price. Similarly, if you have extra Token which you do not wish to use, you can sell them in the market and make money.
Click the arrows to see the idea summarized:

1. Problem
Road traffic and congestion are major global challenges, causing significant costs at individual, environmental, economic, and social levels (Eurostat 2018). By 2030, transport emissions may account for 40% of Denmark’s total emissions (Kamp Damgård 2019).
2. Solution
That's where the Tradable Credit Scheme (TCS) comes in. One Token has the value of 1 kg of CO₂eq.
3. Functionality TCS: Allocation
Everyone gets an initial amount of Token, which can be used for commuting. The allocation depends on the emissions of you commuting behavior from the Travel Diary you reported.
4. Functionality TCS: Market
Need more Token for your commute? You can buy them on the market.
Have leftover Token? You can sell them and earn money. The price changes with every transaction.
5. Functionality TCS: Structure
The regulator (e.g.: government) oversees the system, distributes the Token and monitors the market. The travelers can interact with the market and make commuting decisions.
6. Goal
The TCS helps foster sustainable commuting behavior by making travelers aware of their emissions and incentivizing them to choose greener transport modes.

In the experiment you will be faced with the decision of which travel mode (e.g. car, public transport, bicycle, walk) to use for your daily commute on each weekday. You will be provided with information about each mode. Note that this information is for the combined journey (round trip) from home to work and back to home. Other attributes:
Time Total commute time for the day.
Costs These costs reflect both mode-specific expenses (e.g., fuel, tickets) and the value of the time lost using each mode. For example, even though walking and biking are free, you will see a cost associated with them which reflects the cost of 'time' spent walking/biking, the physical effort required, and potential discomfort/ inconvenience, which are factored in as part of the overall cost.
Token Number of tokens needed to use the mode.
Environmental Ranking The number of leaves indicates how environmentally friendly each commuting option is in comparison to the other options available in your choice set.
(default) The mode marked as "default" is the one you stated in your travel diary and will be automatically selected if you do not make an active choice.
At the beginning of each week, you will be provided with a monetary budget and a Token budget. After you make a travel decision for the daily commute on each day, your monetary budget will be reduced by an amount equal to cost of the mode you selected. Similarly, the Token budget will be reduced by the amount of Token that you used for the selected mode (if any). Every time you buy Token in the market money will be used up from this monetary budget and every time you sell Token your money will be added to this monetary budget. Your Token and monetary budget are valid for one week and will be reset at the beginning of the next week.
